A newsletter about attention as an asset class and its impact on the global economy.

Every edition explains how deals, acquisitions, capital movements, stock moves, ownership changes, distribution shifts, competition and long-term value creation move markets and shape economies.

Why should you trust Attention Capital?

Every edition of Attention Capital is built on publicly available evidence.

\n

Why does Attention Capital matter to operators in developed markets?

In developed markets, the industries that depend on attention are converging quickly.

\n

Media companies are becoming commerce businesses. Sports leagues are becoming entertainment platforms. Retailers are becoming media networks. Gaming companies are becoming social environments. AI companies are changing content production. Advertising, streaming, creator platforms, live events and intellectual property are increasingly competing for the same capital, audiences and distribution channels.

\n

Attention Capital helps operators understand these shifts as one connected business system.

Why does Attention Capital matter to operators in emerging markets?

Many of the companies shaping the global attention economy are built in developed markets, but the lessons travel.

\n

Media, sport, gaming, entertainment, advertising, retail, streaming, creator platforms, AI, live events and intellectual property are all being reshaped by capital, ownership, distribution and monetisation.

\n

Attention Capital helps operators in emerging markets understand the business logic behind these shifts, not just the companies or products making headlines.

\n

The opportunity is not to copy what exists elsewhere. It is to understand how attention-driven businesses create value, then build for the needs, constraints and opportunities of their own markets.
