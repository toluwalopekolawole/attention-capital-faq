What is Attention Capital?

Attention Capital is the operator's manual to the global attention economy.

Attention has become one of the world's most valuable economic assets, yet it is rarely analysed as one. The industries that depend on attention namely media, technology, entertainment, sport, gaming, advertising and retail are typically covered in isolation, with little focus on the capital, ownership and market forces that connect them.

Attention Capital exists to close that gap.

Rather than reporting the news, we use it as raw material to examine the deeper questions that shape attention-driven businesses. Who captures value? Who controls distribution? Where is pricing power emerging? How is ownership changing? And what do these shifts mean for founders, investors, executives and operators?

Every edition is carefully curated to connect developments across industries, identify patterns that others miss, and provide a clearer understanding of how the global attention economy is evolving.

Attention Capital is published every weekday at 09:00 GMT+1 and is free to read.

Why should you trust Attention Capital?

Every edition of Attention Capital is built on publicly available evidence.

We draw from company filings, earnings calls, investor presentations, regulatory disclosures, industry reports and reporting from leading global publications. Every argument is grounded in information that readers can verify for themselves.

Our role is not to break the news. It is to connect developments across industries, identify the commercial patterns they reveal, and explain what they mean for capital, ownership and competition.

You don't have to agree with every conclusion. But you should always be able to understand how we arrived at it.

Why subscribe?

The attention economy is too important to understand one headline at a time.

Every day, developments across media, entertainment, technology, sport, gaming, advertising and retail reshape how attention is created, distributed and monetised. Most are reported as isolated events. Few are examined as part of the same economic system.

Attention Capital brings those developments together.

Every weekday, we curate the most important developments across the global attention economy and examine what they reveal about capital, ownership, competition and long-term value creation. The objective isn't simply to help you keep up with the news. It's to help you develop better judgment about the markets you're operating in.

Over time, our hope is that you don't just become more informed. You become better equipped to recognise opportunities, question assumptions and make decisions in an economy where attention increasingly determines competitive advantage.

Why does Attention Capital matter to operators in developed markets?

Developed markets are where the economics of the attention economy are changing fastest.

AI is reducing the cost of creating content. Distribution is becoming more competitive. As a result, competitive advantage is shifting towards assets that are harder to replicate: trusted brands, owned audiences, intellectual property, proprietary data and resilient distribution.

These are not simply changes in technology. They are changes in how value is created, defended and priced.

Attention Capital examines these shifts through the lens of capital. Every edition connects developments across the attention economy to explain what they mean for competition, ownership and long-term enterprise value.

For operators and investors in developed markets, understanding these changes is becoming less about keeping up with the news and more about recognising how the market itself is evolving.

Why does Attention Capital matter to operators in emerging markets?

Many of the businesses shaping the global attention economy are built in developed markets. But the lessons they offer are not limited to those markets.

Attention Capital is written to help operators understand the underlying economics behind global developments, then apply those insights to the realities of their own markets.

Rather than encouraging readers to replicate what works elsewhere, we examine why it works. The commercial models, ownership structures, competitive dynamics and capital decisions that create long-term value often travel further than the products themselves.

For operators in emerging markets, the opportunity is not to build the next version of someone else's business. It's to use a deeper understanding of the global attention economy to build businesses that reflect the needs, constraints and opportunities of their own markets.
